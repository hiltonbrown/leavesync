## Design Context

### Users

Three overlapping user types, each with different needs:

- **HR admins / ops managers** — configure connections, manage feeds, review sync status. High data density is acceptable; they need control and confidence.
- **Team managers** — check who's out across their team. Scanability and calendar views are primary. They visit frequently, often briefly.
- **Employees** — self-service visibility into their own leave. Surfaces they see should be lighter, less dense, more approachable.

The interface must serve all three without making any group feel like a second-class citizen.

### Brand Personality

**Modern. Calm. Precise.**

LeaveSync is a tool people trust with real business data. It should feel like a well-made instrument — composed, reliable, purposeful. It does not try to entertain. It does not overwhelm. Every screen should lower cognitive load, not raise it.

The emotional goal is **quiet confidence**: the user arrives, sees what they need, acts, and leaves. No friction, no noise.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Primary brand color**: `#336A3B` — a deep forest green. This is the action color, the status signal, the identity anchor. It is used purposefully for interactive elements, confirmation states, and brand moments — not as decoration.
- **Tone**: Clean, spacious, light-first. Professional without being cold.
- **Typography**: Geist Sans (already in stack) — geometric, modern, neutral. Lean into its precision.
- **Radius**: `0.625rem` (already set) — subtly rounded, not bubbly.
- **Motion**: Purposeful and fast. Accordion/transition animations should feel snappy. No decorative motion.
- **Mode**: Light-first. Dark mode is user-preference and should receive equal care.
- **Anti-reference: Notion** — avoid the flat document aesthetic, the undifferentiated text-heavy layout, the lack of visual hierarchy, and the low-contrast UI chrome.

### Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness** — Calendar and absence data must be immediately scannable. Visual hierarchy is non-negotiable. When in doubt, simplify.

2. **Green as signal, not wallpaper** — `#336A3B` earns its place on screen. Use it for primary actions, success states, and brand anchors. Resist applying it broadly as background color or decoration.

3. **Density is role-appropriate** — Admin and manager views can be denser. Employee-facing surfaces should breathe. Never sacrifice readability for compactness.

4. **Calm confidence through space** — Whitespace is load-bearing. Crowded layouts erode trust. Generous padding and clear separation between sections are defaults, not luxuries.

5. **WCAG 2.2 AA is the floor** — All text, interactive elements, and status indicators must meet contrast requirements. Colour is never the sole differentiator for status.
